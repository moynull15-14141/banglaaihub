## Community Strategy Philosophy

The community is not our marketing channel. The community is the product.

Every strategy decision in this document should be evaluated against one question: **"Does this make the Bangla AI community stronger?"** — not "Does this grow our user count?" or "Does this help our metrics?"

Strong communities are built on trust, shared purpose, and genuine mutual benefit. They are not built on growth hacks.

---

## Target Community Profile

### Who We Want

**The Passionate Contributor**
A developer, researcher, or student who genuinely cares about Bangla AI and wants to contribute. They don't need to be incentivized — they need a platform worthy of their contributions.

**The Curious Learner**
Someone starting their AI journey who wants to learn in a way that's relevant to their Bangla context. They need encouragement, guidance, and a welcoming community.

**The Experienced Expert**
A researcher or senior professional who has knowledge to share but needs the right platform to make it discoverable and impactful.

**The Institution**
A university, company, or organization that sees the value of contributing to and collaborating within the Bangla AI ecosystem.

### Geographic Priority
1. Bangladesh (primary — Dhaka, Chittagong, Sylhet, Rajshahi)
2. West Bengal, India (Kolkata, regional areas)
3. Bangladeshi diaspora (UK, USA, Canada, Middle East, Australia)
4. Global Bangla AI researchers (any country)

## Community Architecture

### The Participation Ladder

We want members to gradually increase their engagement. Each step up the ladder increases their investment in the community.

```
Step 1: VISITOR
Browse resources, read discussions
No account required
Goal: Discover value, feel welcome

Step 2: MEMBER
Create account, save resources
Join discussions, ask questions
Goal: Find their people, get help

Step 3: CONTRIBUTOR
Publish resources (datasets, tutorials, etc.)
Contribute to discussions consistently
Goal: Be recognized, give back

Step 4: VERIFIED CONTRIBUTOR
Passes quality review
Community-recognized expert
Goal: Build professional reputation

Step 5: COMMUNITY LEADER
Moderates forum sections
Mentors others, organizes events
Leads regional chapters
Goal: Shape the community

Step 6: PARTNER
Organizations, universities, companies
Formally partnered with Bangla AI Hub
Goal: Build the ecosystem together
```

### Community Spaces

**1. Main Forum**
Topics: General Discussion, Q&A, Resources, Research, Projects, Career, Off-Topic

**2. Dataset Hub**
Discussions specifically around Bangla datasets — sharing, quality, gaps, needs

**3. Research Corner**
Academic discussions, paper reviews, research collaboration requests

**4. Learning Lounge**
Beginner-friendly space — no question is too basic here

**5. Builder's Space**
Show your work, get feedback, find collaborators

**6. Regional Chapters (Online)**
- Bangladesh Chapter (Dhaka + all regions)
- West Bengal Chapter
- UK Diaspora Chapter
- North America Chapter

## Community Growth Strategy

### Phase 1 Seeding (Year 1, 0-1,000 members)

**Goal:** Find and recruit the first 100 committed community members

**Step 1: The Founding 50**
Personally recruit 50 people who are already doing meaningful work in Bangla AI:
- University professors doing NLP research
- Developers who have built Bangla datasets
- Active contributors to Bengali Wikipedia
- Bangla AI Twitter/X community members
- HuggingFace contributors with Bangla models

These 50 people will help seed the first content, moderate early forums, and act as community validators.

**Step 2: Content Seeding**
Before public launch, manually curate 200+ high-quality resources so the platform has immediate value for Day 1 visitors.

**Step 3: Launch Channels**
- Bangla AI groups on Facebook (major channels)
- LinkedIn posts targeting Bangladeshi tech community
- Twitter/X in Bangla AI community
- Reddit (r/MachineLearning, r/LanguageTechnology, r/bangladesh)
- HuggingFace community
- Direct outreach to 20 Bangladeshi universities
- Tech events in Dhaka

**Step 4: Community Loop Activation**
Once 100 members are active, the community begins to grow itself through:
- Word of mouth from satisfied users
- Google search traffic (SEO)
- Shared resources appearing on GitHub, LinkedIn
- University referrals

---

### Phase 2 Growth (Year 2, 1,000-10,000 members)

**Goal:** Build self-sustaining community momentum

**Regular Events**
- Monthly "Bangla AI Showcase" — members present their work
- Weekly "Ask Me Anything" sessions with experts
- Quarterly "Dataset Challenge" — community creates a specific type of dataset
- Annual Bangla AI Summit (virtual, free)

**Student Engagement Program**
- Partner with 25 universities
- Campus ambassador program (1 ambassador per university)
- Student research showcases
- Internship connections through the platform

**Content & Knowledge Programs**
- "Learn AI in Bangla" series — weekly tutorial
- "Bangla AI Paper Review" — simplify research papers for practitioners
- "Dataset of the Month" — spotlight high-quality community datasets

**Recognition Programs**
- Monthly contributor spotlight
- Annual "Bangla AI Champion" awards
- Leaderboard for top contributors

---

### Phase 3 Depth (Year 3+, 10,000-50,000 members)

**Goal:** Build an ecosystem with genuine economic and social value

**Regional Chapter Programs**
Physical meetups, local events, and regional leadership

**Mentorship Program**
Match experienced practitioners with learners

**Research Collaboration Network**
Formalized system for finding research partners

**Creator Cohorts**
Cohort-based creator programs to help people build and publish high-quality datasets and resources

## Community Standards

### Code of Conduct Summary
1. Be respectful — disagreement is fine, disrespect is not
2. Be accurate — don't share misinformation about AI
3. Give credit — always attribute others' work properly
4. Contribute honestly — no plagiarism, no data fabrication
5. Help others — experienced members should support newcomers
6. Speak in Bangla or English — no language discrimination
7. No spam or self-promotion without contribution
8. Report problems — use the reporting system

### Moderation Philosophy
- Assume good intent first
- Warn before punishing
- Context matters — a newcomer mistake vs. repeat violation
- Community moderators are trusted volunteers, not enforcement agents
- Escalation path: Warning → Temporary suspension → Permanent ban

## Community Anti-Patterns to Avoid

**Anti-Pattern 1: Engagement at all costs**
Not every user engagement is good. Low-quality contributions, spam, and controversy-farming hurt the community. We will sometimes choose less activity to preserve quality.

**Anti-Pattern 2: Founder hero worship**
The community should not revolve around the founder. As the platform grows, community leaders should be foregrounded. The founder's job is to build conditions, not to be the center of everything.

**Anti-Pattern 3: Moving too fast**
Community trust takes time to build and seconds to destroy. We will not rush to scale before the community culture is healthy.

**Anti-Pattern 4: Neglecting the core users for growth**
Don't abandon the dedicated early contributors in pursuit of casual mass users. Early contributors are the backbone.
