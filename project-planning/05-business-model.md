## Business Model Philosophy

Bangla AI Hub must be financially sustainable to fulfill its long-term mission. But sustainability does not mean profit maximization. Our business model must:

1. **Never compromise the mission** for revenue
2. **Keep core features free** for the community
3. **Reward creators** who build the ecosystem
4. **Grow sustainably** rather than chasing growth at all cost

We follow the **Open Core** model — the core platform is free and open, while premium features and services generate revenue to sustain operations.

---

## The Business Reality

**Year 1-2: Pre-Revenue**
Building an ecosystem takes time before it generates money. We will operate on:
- Founder bootstrap / savings
- Grants (technology grants, open-source funding)
- Community donations (GitHub Sponsors, Open Collective)
- Potential angel investment if needed

**Year 3+: Revenue Stage**
Once we have a critical mass of users and resources, monetization becomes natural:
- Premium subscriptions
- Marketplace commission
- Institutional licensing
- API access tiers

## Revenue Streams (Full Model)

### Stream 1: Free Tier (Forever Free)
**What's free:**
- Browse and search all resources
- Basic user profile
- Community forums
- Submit resources (up to limit)
- Read all public datasets
- Join community discussions

**Why free:** The community cannot grow if basic access costs money. Free access is how we build trust and scale.

---

### Stream 2: Pro Subscription (Year 2)
**Price:** ~$10-15/month or ~$80-100/year (to be validated)

**What Pro includes:**
- Unlimited resource submissions
- Priority review for submissions
- Advanced search filters
- Saved search alerts (get notified of new resources)
- Higher API rate limits
- Private bookmarks and collections
- Pro badge on profile
- Early access to new features

**Target users:** Researchers, serious developers, active contributors

**Revenue model:**
- Monthly recurring revenue
- High retention (annual plans discounted 30%)

---

### Stream 3: Organization Accounts (Year 2-3)
**Price:** ~$50-200/month depending on size

**What Organization includes:**
- Organization profile page
- Multiple team members
- Dataset licensing management
- Priority listing in directory
- Partnership badge
- Bulk API access
- Analytics dashboard

**Target customers:** Universities, research labs, AI startups, NGOs

---

### Stream 4: Creator Marketplace (Year 3)
The most important long-term revenue stream — and the one that most directly benefits the community.

**How it works:**
- Creators list premium resources (datasets, prompt packs, templates, trained models)
- Buyers pay to access premium resources
- Platform takes 20-30% commission
- Creators keep 70-80%

**Why this matters:**
- Creates economic incentive for high-quality contribution
- Self-reinforcing: more quality creators → more quality buyers → more revenue for everyone

**Examples of premium resources:**
- Specialized Bangla NLP dataset (e.g., medical, legal, conversational)
- High-quality prompt pack for Bangla content creation
- Fine-tuned Bangla language model
- Bangla OCR training dataset
- Annotated Bangla speech dataset

**Revenue target (Year 3):** $2,000/month in marketplace GMV
**Revenue target (Year 5):** $100,000+/month in marketplace GMV

---

### Stream 5: Institutional Data Licensing (Year 3-4)
**Target:** AI companies (like Anthropic, Google, Meta, Cohere), national universities, research labs

**What they buy:**
- Licensed access to curated Bangla dataset collections
- Custom dataset compilation services
- Research partnership packages

**Why they pay:**
- Bangla training data is scarce and valuable
- Building high-quality Bangla datasets is expensive
- The platform's community curation reduces their cost

**Revenue model:** Annual contracts ($5,000 - $100,000+)

---

### Stream 6: API Access Tiers (Year 2-3)
**What we offer:**
- Free tier: 1,000 API calls/month
- Developer tier: 50,000 calls/month ($30/month)
- Business tier: 500,000 calls/month ($200/month)
- Enterprise: Unlimited (custom pricing)

**Use cases:**
- Apps that search Bangla AI resources
- Internal tools at companies
- Research automation
- Integration with other platforms

---

### Stream 7: Grants & Funding (Ongoing)
**Sources to pursue:**
- Mozilla Foundation (open internet, language diversity)
- Open Knowledge Foundation
- Wikimedia Foundation (Bangla Wikipedia connection)
- Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation (South Asia digital inclusion)
- Google.org
- Bangladesh government digital initiatives
- World Bank digital development grants
- Academic research grants (partnered with universities)

**Strategy:**
- Pursue 3-5 grants per year
- Target $10,000-$100,000 per grant
- Use grants for specific programs (dataset creation, community events)

## Creator Economy Model

This is our most important long-term commitment.

### Creator Revenue Share
- Standard: 70% to creator, 30% to platform
- Verified Creator: 75% to creator, 25% to platform
- Founding Creator (first 100): 80% to creator, 20% to platform

### Creator Recognition Tiers

| Tier | Requirement | Benefits |
|------|-------------|----------|
| Contributor | 1+ published resource | Profile badge, community access |
| Active Contributor | 10+ resources, 6 months | Priority in search, newsletter feature |
| Verified Creator | Quality review passed | Marketplace access, 75% revenue share |
| Elite Creator | Top 1% by downloads | Featured placement, 80% revenue share, early access |
| Partner Creator | Organizational partnership | Custom benefits |

---

## What We Will Never Monetize

| Thing | Why We Won't |
|-------|-------------|
| User data | Trust is everything |
| Core resource discovery | Community can't afford gates |
| Basic community access | Inclusion is a value |
| Advertising on core pages | Conflicts with editorial independence |
| Paid ranking in search | Would corrupt the discovery experience |

---

## Funding Strategy

**Phase 1 (2026): Bootstrap + Grants**
- Founder-funded for initial infrastructure
- Apply to 3 open internet/language diversity grants
- Launch GitHub Sponsors for community donations

**Phase 2 (2027): Revenue + Optional Angel**
- First paid tiers launched
- If growth needs capital injection, seek mission-aligned angels
- No VC that requires exit-focused return

**Phase 3+ (2028+): Self-Sustaining**
- Platform revenues cover all operations
- Surplus goes to creator payouts and platform improvements
- Endowment fund for long-term stability
